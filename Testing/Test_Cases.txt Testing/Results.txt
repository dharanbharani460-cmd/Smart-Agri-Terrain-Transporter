Test Case 1: Load Carrying Test
Objective: To check whether the cart can carry agricultural loads.
Procedure: Place a load of crops in the cargo tray and move the cart on flat ground.
Expected Result: The cart should carry the load smoothly without instability.

Test Case 2: Hill Climbing Test
Objective: To check the ability of the cart to move on sloped terrain.
Procedure: Operate the cart on a small inclined surface.
Expected Result: The gear reduction system should provide enough torque to climb the slope.

Test Case 3: Rough Terrain Test
Objective: To test movement on muddy or uneven surfaces.
Procedure: Run the cart on rough ground conditions.
Expected Result: The traction wheels should maintain grip and move the cart forward.

Test Case 4: Stability Test
Objective: To verify the stability of the cargo tray during movement.
Procedure: Drive the cart with crops loaded and observe the tray stability.

Expected Result: The cargo tray should remain stable and prevent crop damage.

The Smart Agri-Terrain Transporter was tested under different conditions such as load carrying, hill climbing, and rough terrain movement.

The cart successfully transported crops without instability. The gear reduction system provided sufficient torque for climbing slopes. The traction wheels allowed smooth movement on uneven and muddy surfaces.

Overall, the prototype demonstrated improved efficiency in transporting crops and reduced manual effort for farmers.
